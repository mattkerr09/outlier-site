§ 02 — Why Outlier

Built the way
Mac software used to feel.

/ 01

8.8 MB, not 200.

Tauri + Rust, not Electron. The installer fits in the space a JavaScript framework wastes on hello world. Native performance, native feel, instant launch.

/ 02

Zero per token. Forever.

Inference runs on your Mac's GPU. No API bills, no usage meters, no "free tier" that expires. Base weights are Apache 2.0. The app is free.

/ 03

Offline by default.

Airplane mode. Coffee-shop Wi-Fi. Secure facility. Your prompts, files, and context never leave the machine. Privacy isn't a toggle — it's the architecture.

/ 04

Claude-speed on Mac.

55 tok/s on the Code tier. 71 tok/s on Lite. Fast enough to feel interactive for agentic work — not a toy demo, not a tech preview. Working software.

/ 05

Honest benchmarks.

Every number has a provenance note: the command, the sample size, the date. No cherry-picked averages. No "up to" marketing math. No bullshit.

/ 06

No lock-in.

Base weights are open. Apache 2.0. You can always walk. The Outlier bet is the platform — tiering, agents, tooling, polish — is worth staying for.

§ 03 — Research Track

A second track: our own MoE.

The shipping app runs curated open-weights today. In parallel, we train Outlier's own ternary-MoE family. Three provisional patents filed.

Ternary-quantized MoE, built as residual deltas over a frozen base.

Our architecture layers ternary {−1, 0, +1} expert weights on top of a full-precision FFN. Target metric isn't raw MMLU — it's MMLU per GB of RAM. That's the axis where a solo team can beat labs with a thousand GPUs.

When a research scale validates against a real Mac tier's RAM budget, it graduates into the shipping stack. Until then, research stays research and the product stays honest.
